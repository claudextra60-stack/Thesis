What is Development of Surfaces?

When a 3-D solid surface is cut along one edge (seam) and laid completely flat, the resulting 2-D shape is called its Development or flat pattern.

This is fundamental to sheet metal work, HVAC ducting, packaging design and fabrication — anywhere a flat sheet must be bent into a 3-D shape.

THE THREE STANDARD METHODS

As taught in all B.Tech Engineering Drawing syllabi (N.D. Bhatt standard)
① PARALLEL-LINE
For Prisms & Cylinders
  • All lateral edges are parallel
  • S.O.L = total perimeter of cross-section
  • Each face becomes a rectangle
  • Models 1–10 in this lab
② RADIAL-LINE
For Pyramids & Cones
  • All edges radiate from apex O
  • Sector radius = slant height l
  • Sector angle θ = (R/l) × 360°
  • Models 11–19 in this lab
③ TRIANGULATION
For Oblique & Transition Pieces
  • Surface divided into triangles
  • True Length (TL) found for every edge
  • Used when no parallel/radial lines exist
  • Models 20–30 in this lab
